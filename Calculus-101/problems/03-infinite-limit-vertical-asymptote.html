3. Infinite Limit Near a Vertical Asymptote3. 垂直漸近線附近的無窮極限

Problem題目

Consider \(f(x)=\dfrac{1}{(x-2)^2}+1\). The denominator is squared, so it is positive on both sides of \(x=2\). As \(x\) gets very close to 2, the denominator becomes very small, and \(\dfrac{1}{(x-2)^2}\) grows without bound.考慮函數 \(f(x)=\dfrac{1}{(x-2)^2}+1\)。分母是平方，因此在 \(x=2\) 兩側都為正。當 \(x\) 非常接近 2 時，分母變得很小，\(\dfrac{1}{(x-2)^2}\) 會無界增大。

The graph has a vertical asymptote at \(x=2\). Read the behavior from both sides and evaluate此圖形在 \(x=2\) 有垂直漸近線。請由左右兩側判讀，並求

\[ \lim_{x\to 2}\left(\frac{1}{(x-2)^2}+1\right) \]

Use the graph first, then justify algebraically why both one-sided limits are positive infinity.先用圖形判讀，再用代數說明為何兩個單邊極限都趨向正無窮大。

What the graph shows圖形說明

Quantity量 Value值 Description說明
Asymptote漸近線 \(x=2\) The denominator is zero at \(x=2\), so the graph has a vertical asymptote there.在 \(x=2\) 分母為零，因此圖形在此有垂直漸近線。
Left limit左極限 \(+\infty\) As \(x\to 2^{-}\), values increase without bound.當 \(x\to 2^{-}\)，函數值無界增大。
Right limit右極限 \(+\infty\) As \(x\to 2^{+}\), values also increase without bound.當 \(x\to 2^{+}\)，函數值同樣無界增大。
Two-sided limit雙邊極限 \(+\infty\) Both one-sided limits are \(+\infty\), so the limit diverges to positive infinity.兩個單邊極限皆為 \(+\infty\)，因此雙邊極限發散到正無窮大。

Concepts you need解題所需概念

  • Infinite-limit notation. Writing \(\lim_{x\to a}f(x)=+\infty\) does not mean the limit is a number. It is shorthand for "the outputs grow without bound"; strictly, the limit does not exist (it diverges).無窮極限記號。寫 \(\lim_{x\to a}f(x)=+\infty\) 並不表示極限是某個數，而是「輸出無界增大」的簡記；嚴格而言，此極限不存在（發散）。
  • Vertical asymptote. A line \(x=a\) that the graph hugs as it shoots off to \(\pm\infty\). It appears where the denominator \(\to 0\) while the numerator stays nonzero.垂直漸近線。圖形衝向 \(\pm\infty\) 時所貼近的鉛直線 \(x=a\)。它出現在分母 \(\to 0\) 而分子不為零之處。
  • The denominator's sign sets the direction. A small positive denominator pushes the quotient to \(+\infty\); a small negative one pushes it to \(-\infty\). Always check the sign on each side.分母的正負決定方向。分母為很小的正數時，商趨向 \(+\infty\)；為很小的負數時，趨向 \(-\infty\)。務必檢查每一側的正負號。
  • A squared factor is never negative. \((x-2)^2\gt 0\) for all \(x\neq 2\), so the fraction is positive on both sides. That is why both one-sided limits are \(+\infty\) here.平方項恆非負。對所有 \(x\neq 2\)，\((x-2)^2\gt 0\)，故分數在兩側皆為正。這正是此處兩個單邊極限都是 \(+\infty\) 的原因。
  • Adding a constant cannot tame infinity. The \(+1\) shifts the graph up by one unit but a term heading to \(+\infty\) still heads to \(+\infty\).加常數無法馴服無窮。\(+1\) 只是把圖形上移一單位；趨向 \(+\infty\) 的項仍然趨向 \(+\infty\)。

Method: find where the denominator is zero; confirm the numerator is nonzero there; decide the denominator's sign just to the left and just to the right; conclude \(+\infty\) or \(-\infty\) on each side.解題步驟：找出分母為零之處；確認分子在該處不為零；判斷分母在左、右兩側的正負號；據此在每一側下 \(+\infty\) 或 \(-\infty\) 的結論。

Task練習

Evaluate the limit:求下列極限：

\[ \lim_{x\to 2}\left(\frac{1}{(x-2)^2}+1\right) \]

State the one-sided limits and then the two-sided limit.請先寫出單邊極限，再寫出雙邊極限。

Hint:提示： Because \((x-2)^2\gt 0\) for \(x\neq 2\), the term \(\dfrac{1}{(x-2)^2}\) is always positive. As \(x\) gets closer to 2, that denominator shrinks toward 0, so the fraction becomes arbitrarily large.由於 \((x-2)^2\gt 0\)（對 \(x\neq 2\)），所以 \(\dfrac{1}{(x-2)^2}\) 永遠為正。當 \(x\) 越接近 2，分母越接近 0，分數就會變得任意大。
Show solution顯示解答

Given已知

\[ f(x)=\frac{1}{(x-2)^2}+1 \]

For \(x\neq 2\), \((x-2)^2\) is positive, and as \(x\to 2\) from either side \((x-2)^2\to 0^{+}\), so當 \(x\neq 2\) 時 \((x-2)^2\) 為正；且 \(x\) 從左右接近 2 時 \((x-2)^2\to 0^{+}\)，因此

\[ \frac{1}{(x-2)^2}\to +\infty \]

Adding 1 does not change this infinite behavior, therefore both one-sided limits diverge:再加上 1 不會改變此無窮趨勢，所以兩個單邊極限皆發散：

\[ \lim_{x\to 2^{-}} f(x)=+\infty, \qquad \lim_{x\to 2^{+}} f(x)=+\infty \]

Since both sides agree, the two-sided limit is由於兩側一致，雙邊極限為

\[ \lim_{x\to 2} f(x)=+\infty \]

So \(x=2\) is a vertical asymptote and the limit is infinite.所以 \(x=2\) 是垂直漸近線，且此極限為無窮極限。
