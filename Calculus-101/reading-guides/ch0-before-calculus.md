# Ch 0  Before Calculus  (pp. 1-48)

Precalculus foundation. Establishes the function concept and the standard
catalog of functions used throughout the course. Best treated as fast review plus
a diagnostic; engineering students vary widely in precalc fluency.

---

## 0.1  Functions  (pp. 1-14)

- **Core concepts:** definition of a function; four representations (numerical,
  algebraic, graphical, verbal); independent vs dependent variable; domain and
  range; natural (implied) domain; graph of a function.
- **Key definitions & theorems:** Definition of a function (each input gives
  exactly one output, 0.1.1 / 0.1.2); domain and range; the **Vertical Line
  Test**; piecewise-defined functions; absolute value function and its
  piecewise form.
- **Prerequisites:** basic algebra, coordinate plane, interval notation.
- **Learning objectives:** students can decide whether a relation is a function;
  find the natural domain and range; evaluate and interpret $f(x)$; read values
  off a graph; apply the vertical line test.
- **Problem types / techniques:** find natural domains (square roots,
  denominators), evaluate $f$ at points and expressions, sketch and interpret
  graphs, piecewise evaluation.
- **Common pitfalls:** forgetting domain restrictions from denominators and even
  roots; confusing $f(x+h)$ with $f(x)+h$; treating a sideways parabola as a
  function.
- **Tech:** optional graphing calculator for visualizing graphs.
- **Tags:** `#functions` `#concept` `#review-precalc` `#foundational`
- **Unit-pick note:** 1 session. Core gateway concept. Pair with a diagnostic.

## 0.2  New Functions from Old  (pp. 15-26)

- **Core concepts:** arithmetic combinations of functions; composition;
  transformations (translations, reflections, stretches/compressions);
  symmetry; even and odd functions.
- **Key definitions & theorems:** sum/difference/product/quotient of functions
  and their domains; composition $f \circ g$, $(f\circ g)(x)=f(g(x))$ and its
  domain; translation/reflection/scaling rules; symmetry tests about the
  $x$-axis, $y$-axis, origin; even function $f(-x)=f(x)$, odd function
  $f(-x)=-f(x)$.
- **Prerequisites:** 0.1.
- **Learning objectives:** students can build composite functions and find their
  domains; predict a graph from transformations of a known graph; classify
  functions as even, odd, or neither.
- **Problem types / techniques:** compute $f\circ g$ and $g\circ f$; decompose a
  function into a composition; graph $y=af(b(x-c))+d$ from a base graph; test
  symmetry.
- **Common pitfalls:** order of composition; domain shrinkage under composition;
  inside vs outside transformations (horizontal shifts go opposite the sign);
  assuming every function is even or odd.
- **Tech:** optional graphing calculator to check transformations.
- **Tags:** `#functions` `#technique` `#visualization` `#review-precalc`
  `#standard`
- **Unit-pick note:** 1 to 2 sessions. Composition is critical setup for the
  chain rule (2.6); do not skip.

## 0.3  Families of Functions  (pp. 27-37)

- **Core concepts:** the idea of a parametrized family; power functions;
  polynomials; rational functions; algebraic functions; the families
  $y=kx^n$, inverse proportionality, and the basic trigonometric family
  $A\sin(Bx+C)$ (amplitude, period, phase shift).
- **Key definitions & theorems:** power function $x^n$ behavior by exponent;
  polynomial degree and end behavior; rational function as ratio of
  polynomials; amplitude, period $2\pi/B$, and phase shift of sinusoids.
- **Prerequisites:** 0.1, 0.2; basic trigonometry (see Appendix B).
- **Learning objectives:** students can recognize and sketch members of each
  family; describe how a parameter changes the graph; read amplitude/period/
  phase from a sinusoid.
- **Problem types / techniques:** identify family from a formula or graph; sketch
  $kx^n$; analyze sinusoidal models; match formulas to graphs.
- **Common pitfalls:** confusing period with frequency; sign of phase shift;
  mixing up odd vs even power behavior.
- **Tech:** graphing calculator useful for exploring parameter effects.
- **Tags:** `#functions` `#visualization` `#review-precalc` `#engineering-application`
  `#standard`
- **Unit-pick note:** 1 session. Sinusoid parameters recur in engineering
  modeling; emphasize them.

## 0.4  Inverse Functions  (pp. 38-48)

- **Core concepts:** one-to-one functions; inverse function; relationship of a
  function and its inverse (reflection across $y=x$); restricting domains to
  obtain inverses; inverse trigonometric functions.
- **Key definitions & theorems:** one-to-one; **Horizontal Line Test**; inverse
  function definition with cancellation equations
  $f^{-1}(f(x))=x$ and $f(f^{-1}(x))=x$; domain/range swap; increasing or
  decreasing functions are one-to-one; definitions and principal ranges of
  $\sin^{-1}$, $\cos^{-1}$, $\tan^{-1}$ (and the other inverse trig functions).
- **Prerequisites:** 0.1, 0.2, 0.3; trig.
- **Learning objectives:** students can test for one-to-one; find an inverse
  algebraically; state domain/range of an inverse; evaluate inverse trig values
  and respect principal ranges.
- **Problem types / techniques:** find $f^{-1}$; verify inverses via cancellation
  equations; graph an inverse by reflection; evaluate $\sin^{-1}$, $\tan^{-1}$,
  compositions like $\cos(\sin^{-1}x)$.
- **Common pitfalls:** assuming every function has an inverse; confusing
  $f^{-1}(x)$ with $1/f(x)$; ignoring principal-range restrictions of inverse
  trig.
- **Tech:** optional.
- **Tags:** `#functions` `#inverse-trig` `#concept` `#review-precalc` `#standard`
- **Unit-pick note:** 1 to 2 sessions. Inverse trig and the inverse concept are
  reused heavily in Ch 6; can be deferred and merged with 6.1/6.7 if time is
  tight.
