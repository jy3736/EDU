# Mascot Usage Guide

The "Yang" mascot is a friendly, bespectacled instructor character used across the
workshop deck to add warmth, signal tone, and guide the reader's eye. This guide
describes each pose, its intended use, and which way it faces so you can pair it
correctly with surrounding text.

> `stust01.png` is **not** a mascot. It is the institutional brand banner
> (Southern Taiwan University of Science and Technology lockup) and is excluded
> from this guide. Do not use it as a character.

---

## Golden rule: the mascot must face the text

**When a mascot is embedded next to a text block, it must face toward (or point
toward) that text, never away from it.** A character looking or gesturing off the
edge of the screen pushes the reader's attention away from the content; a character
turned inward pulls the eye straight into the words.

- Text block is on the **right** of the mascot -> use a **right-facing / right-pointing** pose.
- Text block is on the **left** of the mascot -> use a **left-facing / left-pointing** pose.
- Mascot sits **above** a centered block, or stands alone (no adjacent text) -> a **forward-facing** pose is fine.

If the only pose that fits the message faces the wrong way, **horizontally flip the
image** rather than placing it facing away from the text. (Note: flipping is fine
for these poses because no pose contains readable text on the mascot's own prop
except `yang12` and `yang14`, which must never be flipped.)

## Per-mascot usage

### yang01 — Pointer raised, calm smile
Half body, holding a thin pointer angled to the **upper-left**, neutral friendly
smile.
- **Use for:** section openings, "let's begin", calmly drawing attention to a title
  or heading placed above/left.
- **Faces:** up-left. Pair with a heading on the **left** or directly **below**.

### yang02 — Thinking, "I've got an idea"
Hand on chin, eyes up to the **right**, mouth open in a small aha.
- **Use for:** prompts that pose a question, "think about this", brainstorming, the
  moment before a tip is revealed.
- **Faces:** up-right. Pair with text on the **right**.

### yang03 — Amused / reflecting (close crop)
Tight crop, hand on chin, laughing-thinking expression.
- **Use for:** lighter asides, "good question", reflective notes; works as a small
  inline avatar.
- **Faces:** roughly forward. Use **above** a block or as a standalone avatar.

### yang04 — Presenting with open palm
Open left palm gesturing out to the **right**, welcoming expression.
- **Use for:** "here is...", introducing a figure, table, or example placed to the
  right; the classic presenter pose.
- **Faces:** right. Pair with the introduced content on the **right**.

### yang05 — Celebration, both fists up
Eyes closed, big grin, both fists raised in triumph.
- **Use for:** completion, success, "you did it!", end-of-lab wrap-ups, results that
  went well.
- **Faces:** forward (symmetric). Place **above** or **beside** a congratulatory line.

### yang06 — Whisper / aside
Hand cupped beside the mouth, leaning in.
- **Use for:** insider tips, "between us", pro-tips, gentle warnings delivered softly.
- **Faces:** forward-left. Pair with the tip on the **left** or **below**.

### yang07 — Pointing right, index finger
Half body, index finger clearly pointing to the **right**.
- **Use for:** "look here", call-outs, directing the reader to the next item or a
  specific element on the right.
- **Faces:** right. Pair with the call-out text on the **right**.

### yang08 — One finger up, "first / note this"
Index finger raised on the **upper-left**, calm smile.
- **Use for:** a single key point, "rule #1", "one important thing", numbered tips.
- **Faces:** up-left. Pair with the point on the **left** or **above**.

### yang09 — Bright idea (light bulb)
Holding a glowing light bulb in the right hand, delighted expression.
- **Use for:** insights, "aha" moments, key takeaways, creative suggestions.
- **Faces:** right (toward the bulb). Pair the insight text on the **right**.

### yang10 — Peeking, pointer up
Emerging from the lower-left edge, holding a thin pointer toward the **upper-right**.
- **Use for:** margin/corner placement, "by the way", pointing up at a title or
  callout from a page corner.
- **Faces:** up-right. Pair with text up and to the **right**.

### yang11 — Growth chart
Pointing at a rising bar chart with an upward trend arrow on the **left**.
- **Use for:** results, improvement, before/after gains, statistics that went up
  (e.g. reliability or scores improving).
- **Faces:** left (toward the chart). Pair data/result text on the **left**.

### yang12 — Steps board (Step 1/2/3)
Pointing with a pen at a stacked "Step 1 / Step 2 / Step 3" board on the **left**.
- **Use for:** procedures, ordered instructions, "follow these steps".
- **Faces:** left. Pair the steps on the **left**. **Do not flip** (the board text
  would mirror).

### yang13 — Checklist
Holding and pointing at a checklist with ticked boxes in the right hand.
- **Use for:** verification, "checkpoints", pre-flight checks, "make sure you have...".
- **Faces:** right (toward the checklist). Pair the checklist text on the **right**.

### yang14 — Question board
Holding a whiteboard with a "?" and sparkles on the **right**.
- **Use for:** posing a question, "what do you think?", quiz prompts, the unknown to
  be solved.
- **Faces:** right. Pair the question text on the **right**. **Do not flip** (the
  board mark would mirror).

## Picking the right mascot quickly

- **Start / intro:** yang01, yang04
- **Think / question:** yang02, yang14
- **Tip / insight:** yang06, yang08, yang09
- **Direct attention:** yang07, yang10
- **Procedure / steps:** yang12
- **Verify / checklist:** yang13
- **Results / growth:** yang11
- **Success / celebrate:** yang05
- **Small inline avatar:** yang03
