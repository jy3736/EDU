# Exercise 06 prompt - Spoken reflection (optional extension)

> Sequential messages in one chat. Send them one at a time; later turns build on the
> earlier ones without repeating the setup.

**Message 1** | set the scene and organize into themes
> I've got a teaching reflection I recorded out loud. It's messy and repeats itself, the file is `data/instructor-spoken-reflection.md`. Can you help me clean it up? First pull out the key points into a few clear themes, and make sure you cover these five: course design, how I brought in AI, how student levels split apart, why amotivation (AM) actually went up, and how I want to adjust next time. Heads-up: this is me talking out loud, there are no numbers in it, so if any percentages or statistics show up in your summary, you made them up, please don't.

**Message 2** | pull out the core insight (building on the summary)
> The part I think matters most is where I talk about "the average maybe mixing two different kinds of students together." Pick that out and tell me in one plain sentence how it connects to the t-test results we computed earlier.

**Message 3** | contrast the directions
> One more thing I'm chewing on: in my course AM went up, and in that earlier survey dataset AM also went up. Why is it still not enough to draw a conclusion just from whether the average moved?

**Message 4** | rewrite, then ask what it dropped (bonus)
> Last, rewrite the reflection into a tidy ~150-word formal paragraph I could drop into a report. Then tell me: what repetition did you just cut or merge? (I want to make sure that, in making it read smoothly, you didn't quietly lose the point that "one group of students was actually left behind.")
